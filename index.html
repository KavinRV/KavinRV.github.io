PhD student at IIT Kharagpur – exploring Large Language Models, Natural Language Processing and Information Retrieval

About

I am a doctoral researcher at the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur with experience in pre‑doctoral and BS‑MS research roles. My work lies at the intersection of large language models, natural language processing and information retrieval, with a particular interest in how information is represented across word and document embeddings. I have explored knowledge‑intensive tasks such as question answering, generative retrieval and dialogue systems, using models such as LLaMA, Phi, Qwen and T5, and incorporating techniques like reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) and adapter‑based fine tuning.

Experience

  • Pre‑doctoral researcher, IIT Kharagpur (May 2024 – Jul 2025) – developed query auto‑suggestion systems using prefix trees, generative models and extreme classification techniques and explored RLHF‑inspired methods to improve robustness.
  • MS thesis, IISER Bhopal (Jun 2023 – May 2024) – researched generative retrieval with document‑aware representations and a curriculum‑based contrastive framework.
  • Research intern, IIT Hyderabad (May 2023 – Sep 2023) – investigated efficient context representation for dialogue systems, building modules to select knowledge and generate responses.

Highlights

  • Proposed Aggregate Semantic Grouping, a technique that uses product quantisation to break token representations into shared semantic components, achieving 99.5 % compression while retaining over 95 % task performance.
  • Introduced document‑aware contrastive learning for generative retrieval; accepted at the GenIR@SIGIR24 workshop.
  • Co‑authored a paper on extreme compression in token representations, presented at EMNLP 2025 findings.
